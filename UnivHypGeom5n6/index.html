Some lecture notes

A few notes I took from lectures UnivHypGeom5 and UnivHypGeom6. Just enough to be able to construct the interactive hyperbolic diagram below.

Point

\( A \equiv [x,y] \)

Line

\( \ell = [a:b:c] \) representing equation \( ax + by = c \)

Point on two lines theorem:

If the lines \( \ell_1 = (a_1:b_1:c_1) \) and \( \ell_2 = (a_2:b_2:c_2) \) are not parallel, then there is a unique point A which lies on them both.

\( A = \ell_1 \ell_2 = [ {b_2 c_1 - b_1 c2 \over a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1}, {a_1 c_2 - a_2 c1 \over a_1 b_2 - a_2 b_1} ] \)

Duality in coordinates theorem:

If \( a \equiv [ x_0, y_0 ] \) then \( a^\perp = ( x_0 : y_0 : 1 ) \)

Quadrance in coordinates theorem:

If \( a_1 \equiv [ x_1, y_1 ] \) and \( a_2 \equiv [ x_2, y_2 ] \) then \( q(a_1, a_2) = 1 - {(x_1 x_2 + y_1 y_2 - 1)^2 \over (x_1^2 + y_1^2 - 1)(x_2^2 + y_2 ^ 2 - 1)} \)
